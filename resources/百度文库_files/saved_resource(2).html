Abstract

National Security in the New Era

The concept of national security has evolved significantly, now encompassing both traditional and non-traditional security threats that are increasingly interconnected.

This study employs comprehensive research methods including literature analysis and systematic synthesis to examine the multidimensional aspects of national security.

The research framework is grounded in the holistic approach to national security, providing a comprehensive perspective on contemporary security challenges.

The study analyzes the strategic significance of safeguarding core national interests including sovereignty, development priorities, and social stability.

Contemporary security challenges include complex issues such as ideological infiltration, cybersecurity vulnerabilities, and emerging biological threats.

Systematic Governance Framework

Legal Framework

The study emphasizes the importance of robust legal protections as the foundation for national security governance.

Technological Empowerment

Technological advancement is identified as a critical enabler for effective security systems and threat detection.

Collaborative Approach

The research highlights the need for multi-stakeholder collaboration across government, private sector and civil society.

The study proposes an integrated governance model that combines legal, technological, collaborative and participatory approaches to address contemporary security challenges.

The research provides valuable theoretical and practical insights for enhancing national security institutions and policies.
